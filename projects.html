Advance Software for Earthquake Resistant Rating System for your building

This advance software for ERRS (Earthquake Resistant Rating System) is designed after 2 year of research during our M.Tech. It is the advance version of RVS (Rapid Visual Screening) and SVA (Simplified Vulnerability Assessment), specially designed for Masonry (Brick and Stone) buildings and RCC Buildings. For the data input, its better to take help from any person such as an architect/Civil person who can understands the terminologies of the questions asked, otherwise by understanding the Pictorial representation above every field, anyone can fill it. The software will calculate on its background based on the input and give an rating for your building from earthquake point of view and will suggest some guidelines in the form of report (particularly designed for your building based on input data) to improve the existing condition of your building. We suggest to go for retrofitting only under experts supervision.

Disaster Risk Assessment (DRA) of Uttarakhand State

Under DRA Project, Disaster Risk Assesment of whole Uttarakhand State were carried out with main focused on Earthquake, Landslide, Flood and Industrial Disaster. There were 5 type of surveys conducted by us at more than 350 locations in Uttarakhand (including Rural, Sub Urban, Urban, Plain, Hill top, Valley, River side, Tourist spots). The details of Surveys are:
Building Vulnerability Survey (27000 building data collected)
Flood Vulnerability Survey (1200 building data collected)
Landslide Survey and Rapid Pictures
Socio Economical Survey (13000 House surveyed)
Industrial Survey (75 Industries and 4 Oil Depos)

Industrial Risk Assessment of Uttarakhand State (under DRA)

Industrial Risk Assessment of the state Uttarakhand conducted under DRA Project, Disaster Risk Assessment project, in which an risk assessment for government assigned 75 industry and 4 oil depos was conducted based on the hazards material or chemical presents in each industrial, their quantity, type of storage, wind direction in various months, maximum and minimum temperature of that area, settlement near by etc factors were considered. And with aloha modeling Fire jet, vapor cloud, Fire pool and Fire ball were finds out, if the some disaster caused due to chemicals presents. Based on the vulnerability curves or clouds, GIS map was made to find out the population under risk and built-up under risk. It is found that some part of few districts like under risk and government should take some mitigation for the same. The risk due to Oil Tankers/Truck with Volatile Material taking to the respective industries is also the Very vulnerable situation and also Dangerous for the area and people from where it is passing through.

Industrial Safety and Emergency Preparedness

Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) jointly with Government of Uttar Pradesh, Economic Advisory Council to the Prime Minister (EAC-PM), Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB), Natural Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) and Chemical &Industrial Disaster Management (CIDM) Board together organised a 3-days seminar on ‘Industrial Safety and Emergency Preparedness’ from 19th-21st June, 2018 in Lucknow.
During the program, Hon’ble UP’s governor-Ram Naik, UP’s Minister of Industrial Development-Satish Mahana, Commissioner, Infrastructure and Industrial Development Department-Anup Chandra Pandey, Deputy Secretary General of FICCI-Nirakar Saxena , UP’s Chief Secretary-Rajiv Kumar Prof.Chandan Ghosh-NIDM, Dr. Anup Chandra Pandey-Commissioner, Infrastructure and Industrial Development Department, Lt. Gen Dr. J R Bhardwaj-Chairman, Chemical & Industrial Disaster Management (CIDM) Board, Mr Anshuman Shukla, ceo /Founder TESRA were present.
A technical session was conducted which focused on various important issues related to prevention, preparedness, mitigation and best practices for ensuring safety in chemical, Petro-chemicals, Natural gas and Petroleum Industries. How Hospitals near the Industrial area should be prepared with. How EOC (Emergency operation Centre) will work etc.
